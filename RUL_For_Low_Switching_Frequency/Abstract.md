# Invention Disclosure: Multi-Physics RUL Prediction for Low-Switching Frequency Inverters

## 1. Title
**System and Method for RUL Prediction via Transient Harmonic Analysis in 8kHz PWM-Driven Systems**

---

## 2. Abstract
This disclosure describes a method for predicting the **Remaining Useful Life (RUL)** of an appliance motor specifically optimized for low carrier frequency inverters (**8 kHz**). Due to the Nyquist limitations of an 8 kHz carrier, the system shifts from direct frequency sweeping to **Transient Edge Analysis** and **Magnetic Slope Tracking**. The model fuses:
1.  **Fundamental Inductive Tracking:** Monitoring the $L/R$ slope within the 800 Hz – 4 kHz band.
2.  **Harmonic Excitation:** Utilizing the 3rd, 5th, and 7th harmonics of the 8 kHz carrier to probe core permeability.
3.  **Transient Ringing Frequency:** Analyzing the high-frequency sub-microsecond "ringing" generated by the 8 kHz switching edges to detect resonance shifts ($\Delta f_r$) without requiring a high-frequency sweep.

---

## 3. Technical Constraints (8 kHz Environment)
*   **The Nyquist Barrier:** Direct impedance measurement is limited to $< 4 \text{ kHz}$.
*   **Switching Noise:** High-amplitude noise at 8 kHz, 16 kHz, and 24 kHz.
*   **Solution:** The invention utilizes the **Step Response** of the PWM pulse. Each 8 kHz "edge" acts as a Dirac-like impulse, exciting high-frequency resonances that are captured via high-speed oversampling.

---

## 4. Key Health Indicators (Modified)

### A. Magnetic Permeability ($\mu$) Tracking
In an 8 kHz system, the current ripple is larger. This is an advantage.
*   **Indicator:** The **Ripple Slope ($di/dt$)** during the "ON" time of the PWM cycle.
*   **Aging Detection:** As the core material ages (permeability decay), the $di/dt$ slope steepens for a given voltage, providing a direct measurement of **Magnetic Core Health**.

### B. Transient Resonance Shift ($\Delta f_r$)
Since we cannot sweep to 200 kHz with an 8 kHz carrier, we use the **Switching Transient**:
*   **Indicator:** The frequency of the **Sub-cycle Ringing** ($f_{ring}$) occurring at the PWM transition.
*   **Health Correlation:** A shift in $f_{ring}$ toward higher frequencies indicates **Insulation Thinning** (reduced stray capacitance) or **Winding Movement**.

---

## 5. Statistical Model Architecture
The RUL is calculated using a **State-Space Model** that weights the following:


| Parameter | Frequency Source | Sensitivity |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Winding Resistance ($R$)** | Fundamental (0 - 1 kHz) | Low (Thermal Indicator) |
| **Permeability ($\mu$)** | PWM Ripple Slope (8 kHz) | **High (Core Aging)** |
| **Resonance ($f_r$)** | Edge Ringing ($>$100 kHz) | **High (Insulation Aging)** |

## 6. Novelty and Advantages
*   **Zero-Hardware Change:** Implementation is entirely in the **firmware/software** layer of existing 8 kHz inverters.
*   **Harmonic Leveraging:** Uses the "imperfections" of low-frequency switching (harmonics and ringing) as a diagnostic tool.
*   **Multi-Physics Correlation:** First model to link 8 kHz ripple characteristics to long-term magnetic core degradation.

---

## 7. Revised Claims
1.  A method for extracting high-frequency resonance shifts from **low-frequency (8 kHz) PWM edges** for the purpose of RUL prediction.
2.  A predictive maintenance system that calculates a **Magnetic Health Index** by analyzing the variation in the **current ripple slope** over the appliance's lifecycle.
3.  The use of **Bayesian filtering** to separate temperature-induced resistance changes from material-induced permeability decay in an 8 kHz inverter drive.
