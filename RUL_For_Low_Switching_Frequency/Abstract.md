# Invention Disclosure: Multi-Physics RUL Estimation in Low Frequency Inverter Systems

---

## 1. Title
**System and Method for Estimating Remaining Useful Life (RUL) of Electric Motors via Ripple-Transient Analysis of Core Material and Electrical Decay in Low Frequency Inverters**

---

## 2. Abstract
This disclosure describes a non-invasive, firmware-based system for predicting the **Remaining Useful Life (RUL)** of an electric motor by correlating its electrical and magnetic material degradation. Specifically optimized for **Low Frequency Inverter** environments where traditional high-speed signal injection is hardware-limited, the invention overcomes the Nyquist constraints of low-speed carriers by utilizing **Intra-Cycle Transient Analysis**.

The system extracts high-fidelity health indicators from two primary physical phenomena:
1.  **Magnetic Core Decay:** The model monitors the **time-domain current ripple slope ($di/dt$)** during the PWM "ON" state to quantify the irreversible loss of **Magnetic Permeability ($\mu$)**.
2.  **Electrical Insulation Decay:** The system captures the sub-microsecond **High-Frequency Edge Ringing** generated by the switching transitions. Shifts in the natural resonance frequency ($f_r$) of these transients are used to detect dielectric aging via parasitic capacitance drift.

These indicators are fused into a unified **Health Index (HI)**. A Bayesian-updated statistical model then projects the HI trajectory to provide a probabilistic RUL estimate. By leveraging the inherent "noise" and ripple of a **Low Frequency Inverter**, the invention provides a "zero-hardware-cost" diagnostic solution for detecting sub-threshold material failure.

---

## 3. Background of the Invention
### 3.1 The "Blind Spot" in Low Frequency Inverters
In modern consumer appliances (HVAC, washing machines, pumps), cost-effective inverters typically operate at low carrier frequencies. This presents a significant diagnostic barrier:
* **The Nyquist Limitation:** Standard controllers operating in a **Low Frequency Inverter** environment cannot "see" high-frequency electrical resonances ($100\text{ kHz}+$) that signal the early stages of insulation thinning.
* **Magnetic Masking:** The fundamental drive current dominates the signal, masking subtle changes in **Core Permeability ($\mu$)** caused by years of thermal and magnetic cycling fatigue.

### 3.2 The Necessity of the Invention
Existing RUL models treat the motor as a static "black box." There is a critical need for a method that extracts material-level health indicators—specifically core decay and resonant shifts—using only the existing hardware of a **Low Frequency Inverter**.

---

## 4. Detailed Description: Ripple-to-Permeability Conversion
The system treats every PWM "ON" pulse as a discrete step-response experiment, turning a hardware limitation into a diagnostic advantage.

### 4.1 Step-by-Step Algorithm
1. **Intra-Cycle Sampling:** High-speed capture of current samples within the active PWM pulse.
2. **Inductance Extraction:** Solving the voltage equation: $V_{dc} - V_{bemf} = L(\mu, i) \frac{di}{dt} + Ri$.
3. **Permeability Normalization:** Calculating the ratio of measured inductance against a commissioning baseline ($L_{0}$) recorded during the motor's "new" state.
4. **Health Scoring:** Converting the permeability ratio into a 1.0 to 0.0 scale, where a steepening ripple slope indicates magnetic core fatigue and loss of flux efficiency.

---

## 5. Detailed Description: Edge Ringing Analysis
In a **Low Frequency Inverter**, the switching edges act as "Broadband Excitation" pulses regardless of the carrier speed.
1. **The Signal:** Each edge triggers high-frequency "Ringing" (oscillations) in the MHz range.
2. **The Measurement:** Sampling at the transition identifies the **Natural Resonance Frequency ($f_r$)**.
3. **Aging Correlation:** As insulation material degrades or undergoes chemical changes, the dielectric constant shifts. A measurable drift in $f_r$ serves as the **Electrical Decay Index**.

---

## 6. Flowchart Process Logic
The firmware operates in three asynchronous loops to manage the constraints of a **Low Frequency Inverter**:
* **High-Speed Loop:** Captures $di/dt$ and Edge Ringing at the microsecond scale.
* **Fusion Loop:** Normalizes data for temperature and load, calculating a multi-physics Health Index.
* **Prediction Loop:** Uses a Bayesian Recursive Filter to update the degradation slope and project the RUL in operational hours.

## 8. Specific Claims
1. A method for determining the health of an electric motor driven by a **Low Frequency Inverter**, comprising calculating a **Magnetic Health Index** derived from the slope of the PWM current ripple.
2. An algorithm that extracts **Resonance Frequency Shifts** from the switching transients of a **Low Frequency Inverter** without external high-frequency signal injection.
3. A predictive system that utilizes a **Bayesian filter** to fuse magnetic and electrical decay scores into a unified RUL estimate.
4. A method as in Claim 1, using **current ripple curvature** ($d^2i/dt^2$) to identify the onset of magnetic saturation as a precursor to motor failure.

## 9. Prior Art and Novelty Analysis

### 9.1 Known Prior Art
This invention acknowledges several existing techniques in motor diagnostics:
*   **MCSA (Motor Current Signature Analysis):** Commonly used for detecting rotor bar breakage but lacks sensitivity to long-term magnetic core material decay.
*   **PWM-Based Parameter Identification:** Techniques such as those described in [US11656286B2](https://patents.google.com/patent/US11656286B2/en) utilize current ripples for real-time inductance ($L$) estimation to improve control loop performance.
*   **High-Frequency Resonance Monitoring:** Academic studies (e.g., [IEEE 1531603](https://ieee.org)) have demonstrated that insulation aging causes shifts in parasitic capacitance ($C_s$), leading to resonance shifts.

### 9.2 Distinguishing Novelty of the Present Invention
The present invention is distinguished from the prior art by the following "Inventive Steps":
1.  **Inverse Use of Low Frequency Artifacts:** While standard inverter controllers treat 8kHz-band ripples and switching transients as noise to be filtered, this invention utilizes them as high-fidelity "material probes."
2.  **Permeability-to-RUL Mapping:** Unlike prior art that identifies Inductance ($L$) for control purposes, this system maps the *decay rate* of $L$ directly to the physical fatigue of core magnetic domains (Permeability Decay), which is a novel prognostic indicator.
3.  **Passive Edge-Ringing Analysis:** The invention captures resonance frequency shifts ($\Delta f_r$) by oversampling the natural "ringing" of the Low Frequency Inverter's switching transitions, eliminating the need for the costly high-frequency signal injection required by existing aging-monitoring patents.
4.  **Multi-Physics Health Fusion:** The unique statistical weighting of core material health (magnetic) and winding dielectric health (electrical) provides a more robust RUL estimate than single-domain models.
